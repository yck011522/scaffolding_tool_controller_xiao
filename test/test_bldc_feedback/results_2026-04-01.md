# test_bldc_feedback — Results 2026-04-01

## Motors Tested

| Gearbox Ratio | Rated Output RPM | No-Load Motor RPM (measured) |
|---|---|---|
| 56:1 | 142 RPM | ~8180 RPM (motor), ~146 RPM (shaft) |
| 169:1 | 47 RPM | ~7980 RPM (motor), ~47 RPM (shaft) |

The two remaining ratios (middle values) were **skipped** — the motor-side RPM curves are nearly identical between the fastest and slowest gearboxes (within 1–2%), confirming the feedback is from the motor before the gearbox. Any calibration data from one motor applies to all four.

## Voltage Divider (CAP Pin)

The CAP output has high source impedance. The original 3.3 kΩ / 4.7 kΩ divider loaded the signal too much, pulling the output below usable levels.

**Final divider:** 22 kΩ (series) + 47 kΩ (to GND) → measured 2.97 V from 5.2 V source. Safe for ESP32 and above the digital HIGH threshold.

## PWM Frequency Selection

All five tested frequencies (5, 10, 15, 20, 25 kHz) produced virtually identical RPM vs duty-cycle curves (within ±20 RPM measurement noise).

**Design choice: 20 kHz**

Rationale:
- **Above audible range** (~20 kHz threshold) — eliminates PWM whine from driver/motor
- The driver's internal RC filter smooths 20 kHz into a very clean DC level
- No performance penalty compared to lower frequencies
- 20 kHz is the industry-standard PWM frequency for motor drives
- 5 kHz is audible and offers no benefit; 25 kHz is unnecessary

## Feed-Forward Model

The relationship between PWM duty cycle and motor RPM is highly linear in the active range (40–90%). The dead band (0–35%) and saturation zone (95–100%) are the only nonlinear regions.

From the sweep data (averaged across frequencies):

```
Motor RPM ≈ 145 × (PWM% − 33)     for PWM ∈ [40, 90]
```

Inverting for feed-forward control:

```
target_PWM% = (target_motor_RPM / 145) + 33
```

To convert from output shaft RPM:

```
target_motor_RPM = target_shaft_RPM × gear_ratio
target_PWM% = (target_shaft_RPM × gear_ratio / 145) + 33
```

### Saturation handling

- **Below dead band (PWM < 40%):** Motor does not spin. Controller should clamp to 0% (stop) or 40% (minimum running speed).
- **Above saturation (PWM > 90%):** Diminishing returns; motor approaches no-load speed (~8200 RPM). Controller should clamp at 100%.
- **Hysteresis:** Motor stops at ~37% when ramping down but requires ~40% to start. A practical minimum is 40%.

## Recommended Speed Controller (for integration step)

A simple **feed-forward + P** controller:

```
pwm = feedforward(target_rpm) + Kp × (target_rpm − measured_rpm)
```

Where:
- `feedforward(target_rpm) = (target_rpm / 145) + 33` (motor RPM)
- `Kp` = proportional gain, to be tuned (start with ~0.01, i.e., 1% PWM per 100 RPM error)
- The feed-forward handles the steady-state mapping; the P term corrects for load variation
- Integral term is likley not needed — the feed-forward is accurate enough, and we don't need accurate zero steady-state error for this application
