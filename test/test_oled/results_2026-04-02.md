# OLED Display Test Results — 2026-04-02

## Hardware

- Display: 0.66″ SSD1306 OLED
- Interface: I2C, address 0x3C (module selector at 0x78)
- Wiring: SDA → GPIO15, SCL → GPIO16, VCC → 3.3 V, GND → GND

> **Pin history:** Original test on XIAO ESP32-S3 used D4/D5 (GPIO5/6) via Grove Shield. On the Waveshare ESP32-S3-Tiny, I2C is on GPIO15/16.

## Key findings

### Framebuffer offset

The SSD1306 has a 128×64 internal framebuffer, but the 0.66″ module only
shows a **64×48 visible window**.  Drawing at (0, 0) puts content off-screen.

Correct offsets (determined experimentally):

| Axis | Offset | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| X | 32 | Content starts at pixel column 32 |
| Y | 16 | Content starts at pixel row 16 |

Driver must be initialised as `Adafruit_SSD1306(128, 64, &Wire, -1)` with
the full buffer size. All `setCursor()` calls use `(32 + col, 16 + row)`.

### Text size legibility

Tested 7 pages cycling every 5 seconds. Visual assessment at normal viewing
distance (~30 cm):

| textSize | Char size (px) | Chars/line | Lines | Verdict |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | 6 × 8 | 10 | 6 | **Legible** — smallest usable size, all characters readable |
| 2 | 12 × 16 | 5 | 3 | **Legible** — good for headers and large numeric readouts |
| 3 | 18 × 24 | 3 | 2 | **Too large** — fills the screen, not practical for status info |

### Recommended layouts for UI design

1. **Full size-1 status screen** (10 chars × 6 lines = 60 chars max):
   Best for dense information display — motor status, sensor readings, etc.

2. **Mixed size-2 header + size-1 body** (1 title line + 4 detail lines):
   Best balance of readability and information density. Title in size 2,
   details in size 1.

3. **Size-2 numeric readout** (5 chars × 3 lines):
   Good for displaying 1–3 large values (e.g. current, speed, voltage).

## Test firmware

The demonstration firmware is saved in `test/test_oled/main.cpp`.  It cycles
through all 7 layout pages and can be re-flashed at any time to revisit the
text-size options during UI design.

## Build info

- Environment: `test_oled`
- Flash: 285 KB (8.5%)
- RAM: 18.9 KB (5.8%)

## Re-test on Waveshare ESP32-S3-Tiny with 0.96″ OLED — 2026-04-09

### Hardware change

Upgraded from 0.66″ (64×48 visible window) to **0.96″ (128×64 full screen)**. Same SSD1306 controller, same I2C address (0x3C), same wiring (GPIO15/16).

### Key difference

The 0.96″ module uses the **full 128×64 framebuffer** — no cursor offset needed. Drawing starts at (0, 0). The old `VIS_X=32` / `VIS_Y=16` offsets have been removed from the firmware.

### Updated text size reference (128×64 full screen)

| textSize | Char px | Chars/line | Lines | Notes |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | 6 × 8 | 21 | 8 | Good for dense status display |
| 2 | 12 × 16 | 10 | 4 | Good for headers and large numeric readouts |
| 3 | 18 × 24 | 7 | 2 | Large values; room for a size-1 footer |
| Mixed 2+1 | — | — | 2 header + 6 body | Best layout for status screens |

### Updated recommended layouts

1. **Full size-1 status screen** (21 chars × 8 lines = 168 chars max) — much more room than the old 60-char limit
2. **Mixed size-2 header + size-1 body** (2 title lines + 6 detail lines)
3. **Size-3 large readout** (7 chars × 2 lines + size-1 footer)

### Result

All 7 test pages display correctly using the full screen. **PASS.**
- Library: Adafruit SSD1306 + Adafruit GFX
